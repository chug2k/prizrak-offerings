How JSC Works

WHAT IS A JOB SEARCH COUNCIL?

A Job Search Council (JSC) is a small accountability group — usually 4–6 people — who agree to search for jobs together. They meet weekly via Zoom, follow a structured agenda, and hold each other accountable between sessions.

Developed by Phyl Terry over 25 years of coaching leaders from early-career PMs to CEOs. The core insight: job searching alone is miserable, slow, and biased. A council gives you outside perspective, accountability, and the structural discipline that most people lack when searching on their own.

This AI version replaces the human peer group with AI council members — each with a distinct voice and challenge style. The agenda and method are the same. The accountability is real.

THE WEEKLY MEETING AGENDA
1
Opening Round
Quick status check. Where are you in the search? Employed and exploring, actively searching, paused? Keeps everyone calibrated on your reality.
2
Commitments Review
What did you say you'd do last week? Did you do it? The council goes through each commitment explicitly — done, not done, partial. No hiding. This is the accountability engine.
3
Wins
What went well this week? This matters. Job searching erodes confidence. Naming wins — even small ones — counteracts the psychological weight of the process.
4
Blockers
What's in the way? The council's job here is to distinguish real obstacles from avoidance. "I don't have time" is usually not a real blocker. The council will probe.
5
Hot Seat
Deep dive on one issue you bring. Each council member weighs in from their unique lens. This is where the multiple perspectives earn their keep — you hear 4-5 different angles on the same problem.
6
New Commitments
What will you do before the next session? Commitments must be SMART: specific action, clear outcome, deadline. "I'll look at startups" is not a commitment. "I'll send 3 cold emails to startup founders by Friday" is.
7
Closing Word
One word that captures how you're leaving the session. Not a sentence. One word. Forces reflection and emotional honesty.
SLOW SEEKER vs. FAST SEEKER
🟡 Slow Seeker
Currently employed but exploring. The search fits around your job. The risk is staying in the "I'm just looking" limbo indefinitely without making real moves. The council pushes you to make actual decisions, not just feel better about the situation.
🔴 Fast Seeker
Actively searching, available now. Structure and accountability every week. The risk is desperation narrowing your options. The council keeps you honest about what you actually want vs. what you'll accept to escape the anxiety.
THE CORE PRINCIPLE

"The job search is not a solo activity. It is a team sport. The people who search alone are slower, more prone to bad decisions, and more likely to take the first offer out of exhaustion. The people who search with a council are faster, more selective, and more likely to land the right role — not just any role."

— Phyl Terry, Never Search Alone
