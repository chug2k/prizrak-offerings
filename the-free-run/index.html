The Free Run

A Saturday with no zeitgeber. No approval to seek, no boss to impress. What does your clock actually want — when nothing is watching but the ghost?

Michel Siffre spent six months in Midnight Cave, Texas. Without daylight, schedules, or clocks, his sleep-wake cycle drifted to 48 hours. His biological rhythm did not collapse — it expanded into its natural form. The zeitgeber (the time-giver) is what makes us conform to 24-hour days. Remove it, and the clock reveals what it actually wanted to do.

This is a zeitgeber-free Saturday. No external signals. No approval loop. Just the question: what would you do if today had no social pressure attached? The cave keeps no record but the one you give it.

QUESTION 01 / THE FIRST

If no one would know what you did today — no one to report to, no one to impress — what would you actually spend the day doing?

QUESTION 02 / THE APPROVAL FILTER

What things on your mental list for today are there because you want them, vs. because someone else's approval is attached to them?

QUESTION 03 / THE HONEST SIGNAL

What is your body actually asking for today? (Battery, stress, sleep debt — ignore the 'should' and read the signal.)

THE ACTUAL DAY

Block the day in honest time. Tag each block: what kind of energy does this require? No pressure to be productive — rest is a legitimate answer. The ledger does not judge. It only remembers.
