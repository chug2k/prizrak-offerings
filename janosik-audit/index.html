From an Oak to an Oak

Who are you robbing? Who are you feeding?

Juraj Jánošík, highwayman of the Tatras, 1688–1713, operated by one rule: "od buka do buku" — from an oak to an oak. Take from the nobleman. Give to the village. He was caught because someone informed on him. He was hanged on a hook through his ribs. He became a national hero anyway. The code outlasted the man.
The audit question: whose oak are you filling this week, and whose village are you feeding?
