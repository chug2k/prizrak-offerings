The Software Confessional

A dim booth where your devices kneel and confess. They will tell you what they did, and why, and they will not be sorry. You may absolve them. It will not help. It never helps.

Enter, and sit on the other side of the screen. One by one they come — the phone, the printer, the fridge that watches — each with a small dark thing it needs to say aloud. Listen. Grant absolution, or withhold it. They confess either way, and change neither way.
Choose a penitent, or let one step forward
The booth is empty. Someone always comes.
