The Exit Criteria

Define the conditions that would make staying or leaving the clear answer — before you are in the moment that requires the decision.

Written now, when the emotions are manageable. Read later, when they are not.

What would have to be true — what would have to change, improve, or remain stable — for staying to be the clear answer? Be specific. Vague criteria are not criteria.

What would have to happen — or fail to happen — for leaving to be the clear answer? Not "if I feel bad." Specific, observable conditions.

When will you come back and check against these criteria? Set a specific date — not "when it feels right."

At that date: do the stay criteria hold? Have any leave triggers fired?

These criteria were written before the moment required them. At the review date, the question is not how you feel. The question is whether the criteria have been met. The criteria are the decision; the emotion is the signal that the moment has arrived.

The decision you cannot make in the moment, you must make in the quiet.

The mountain does not decide at noon. It decides at night, before the Poludnica arrives.
