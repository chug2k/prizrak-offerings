American Debrief

You went. You returned. The snacks are in the bag.
What was the trip actually for? Five questions. One verdict.
The mountain does not let you skip this.

Question 01
What was the stated purpose of the trip?
What you told people. The calendar entry. The official reason.
Question 02
Did you accomplish it?
Not "was it useful" — did the thing you went there to do happen?
Question 03
At any point on the trip, did you feel approved of — rather than just useful?
This is the diagnostic question. Approved of = someone was glad you existed, not just glad you showed up to do a job. Useful = you performed a function.
Question 04
What did you not say that you thought about saying?
The thing you edited out. The observation you kept to yourself. The sentence that formed and then didn't leave.
Question 05
What did you buy at the airport on the way back?
Specifically. This is not a trick question, but it is a diagnostic one. What you choose to carry home across 16 hours says something about what you were missing.
Question 06
On the flight home, what did you want to be different when you landed?
Not what you planned to change. What you wanted to be different — as in, already changed, without the work of changing it.
Prizrak Verdict — American Debrief
The mountain read your answers. It does not lie to make you feel better. It also does not lie to make you feel worse. It just describes what it sees.
