The Fog Walker · Vault

A drowned moor, raised fresh from a seed. A line of stepping-stones glows across the black water — for one held breath. Memorize it. Then the fog comes down and the glow dies, and you must walk the line on memory alone. Step true and the stone holds. Step wrong and the water takes you. A pale wisp — the bludička — drifts ahead with a false lantern; learn to distrust the light. Reach the far shore. Then do it again, longer and darker.
