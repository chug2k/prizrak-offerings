The Science Behind the Protocol

Why east is harder: Your biological clock runs ~24.5h — slightly longer than a day. It naturally prefers delay (staying up later). Traveling east forces advance (earlier sleep). Every morning light exposure pushes the clock forward. Miss the morning window and you lose a day of recovery.
Caffeine and the Poludnica: Caffeine blocks adenosine receptors — the molecule that builds sleep pressure. The afternoon dip (1–3 PM) is real, hard-wired into your circadian system. Coffee at 2 PM defers the dip. It follows you home at 8 PM when the caffeine clears and the accumulated adenosine floods back. Cut off by noon on day 1.
Light as the master reset: The suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) — 20,000 neurons in your hypothalamus — sets your internal clock via light. Bright morning light advances the clock. Evening light delays it. On day 1, seek morning sun like it owes you money. Avoid screens and bright light after 9 PM.
The first night rule: No matter how tired you are, do not sleep before 10 PM Saigon time on day 1. Crashing at 6 PM locks in the US clock. One bad night of social jetlag is recoverable. Three bad nights compound.
Melatonin timing: 0.5 mg taken ~2 hours before target bedtime, on nights 1 and 2. Small dose. Timing matters more than quantity. Higher doses extend into the next morning and make grogginess worse.
